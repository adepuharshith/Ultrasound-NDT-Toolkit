The wavelength scales with wave speed — a faster material yields longer wavelengths at the same frequency. The animation shows the spatial pressure pattern in each medium. Compressions move rightward; the ratio λ₂/λ₁ = c₂/c₁.

Immersion Testing

💧 Water Path Calculator

Must be ≤ material thickness

Required Water Path (WP)
— mm
WP = f − mp × (cₛ / cₚ)

Position the transducer face this distance above the material surface. The longer path in the faster solid "uses up" focal length equivalent to mp × cₛ/cₚ mm of water path.

cₚ default 1495 m/s ≈ water at 23 °C (±3 m/s per °C).

Beam Physics

🔭 Near-Field Distance

Near-Field Distance N
— mm
N = D² / (4λ) = D² ⋅ f / (4c)

Inspect at or beyond N for stable, predictable amplitude response. In the near field, constructive/destructive interference causes irregular amplitude variations that complicate flaw sizing.

Depth Sizing

⏳ Time-of-Flight → Depth

Depth (one-way)
— mm
d = (TOF × c) / 2

Enter the total round-trip time from the A-scan (transducer face to reflector and back). The result is the one-way depth. Divide by 2 because the pulse travels down and back.

Signal Loss

🔴 Attenuation Loss

Total Attenuation
— dB
Loss = α × f × d

Typical α (dB/mm/MHz): steel 0.3–1.5, aluminum 0.2–0.8, CFRP 1–5, HDPE 2–5, water 0.002. Coarse-grained castings can be 10× higher than wrought alloys.

Refraction & Mode Conversion

📈 Snell's Law & Critical Angle

Refracted Angle θ₂
— °
sin(θ₂) = (c₂/c₁) ⋅ sin(θ₁)

Default: water → steel (L-wave). For shear-wave wedge inspection use c₂ = 3200 m/s. “TIR” = total internal reflection (incident angle exceeds first critical angle).

Beam Physics

🔍 Beam Divergence & Spread

Half-angle θ½ (−6 dB)
— °
Full Beam Spread
— °
sin(θ½) = 0.514λ/D    sin(θ) = 1.2λ/D

Half-angle θ½ is from the acoustic axis to the −6 dB pressure edge. Full beam spread = 2 × θ½. Larger diameter or higher frequency concentrates the beam. Near-field distance N is shown for context.
