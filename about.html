About This Toolkit

The Ultrasound & NDT Toolkit is an open, browser-based educational and practical reference for ultrasonic nondestructive evaluation. It was built to bridge the gap between dense textbook theory and day-to-day lab practice — giving students and practitioners quick access to interactive calculators, visual theory explanations, and curated resources, all in one place.

Every calculator and diagram on this site reflects real inspection scenarios encountered in the Purdue NDT Research Lab — from setting water paths for immersion transducers to sizing cracks with TOFD and calibrating phased array setups. The goal is a living reference that grows alongside active research.

All content is freely available. If you use this toolkit in your research or teaching, a mention or link back is appreciated but not required.

Research Focus

My doctoral research sits at the intersection of advanced ultrasonic sensing and data-driven signal analysis, with applications to aerospace and infrastructure inspection.

Phased Array Ultrasound

Delay-law optimisation, S-scan reconstruction, and FMC/TFM imaging for complex geometry inspection.

Composite NDE

Detection of delaminations, impact damage, and manufacturing voids in CFRP and GFRP structures.

Signal Processing

Hilbert transforms, time-gain compensation, pulse compression, and machine-learning assisted flaw classification.

Transducer Characterisation

Beam profiling, near-field mapping, impedance analysis, and wideband calibration methods.
